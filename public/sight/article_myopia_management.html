What exactly is Myopia Management?

Traditional glasses are like a crutch‚Äîthey help you walk, but they don't heal the leg. In nearsightedness, the eye grows too long from front to back. Proper management uses specific optics or medications to signal the eye to stop growing so fast.

"Every diopter of myopia increases the risk of myopic macular degeneration by 58%." ‚Äî Journal of Optometry and Vision Science

The Top 3 Evidence-Based Methods

Clinical studies have verified these three primary treatments:

  • Orthokeratology (Ortho-K): Hard contact lenses worn only at night. They gently reshape the cornea while the child sleeps, providing clear vision during the day without glasses.
  • Atropine Eye Drops: Low-dose drops (typically 0.05%) applied at bedtime. They are highly effective at slowing eye growth with minimal side effects.
  • Dual-Focus Contact Lenses: Specialized daily disposable soft lenses (like MiSight) specifically designed to create peripheral defocus, which stops eye elongation.

Searching for "Near Me"? Here's what to check

When looking for a local clinic, don't just go to any optical shop. Look for practitioners who mention "Fellow of the International Academy of Orthokeratology" or specialize in "Pediatric Optometry."

Important questions to ask your local doctor:

"Do you utilize axial length measurement (Biometry) to track my child's eye growth, or only prescription changes?"
